Q) Why develop this?

Social stigma leads to ~50% of people not seeking help with depression. This makes a privacy-first clinically validated tool critically important for early detection.

Q) What is the source of these questions?

PHQ9. It is a clinically validated depression measurement scale.

Q) Why PHQ9?

In my decade old practice of treating patients with mental health disorders and doing differential analysis on over 1000 patients, I have found PHQ9 to be the most accurate in generating outcomes similar to those arrived by highly trained psychiatrists. In my patients the first tool I use is PHQ9 and educate them about it and ask them to share it with their family and freinds. With each episode of depression the neural pathways get stronger. PHQ9 helps us find depression at early onset and this has allowed me to treat many patients with better outcomes.

Compared to Beck depression inventory scale, PHQ9 has given me better results consistently. It is most probably due to the fact that Beck has 21 questions and most of my depressed patients find it difficult to answer 21 questions in one go.

I am convinced that we can do nothing better than making this tool widely accessible to the masses. PHQ9 is the thermometer of measuring depression and it is a travesty that not many people know about it.

Q) How can I know that it is not taking my information?

All the computations happen inside your browser.
